# Sensitive Scenarios: Mabel Cheung

> This is a synthetic user for AI pre-validation only. It is not a real person and does not replace human market research.

## Identity Disclosure

reaction: She prefers optionality. She is fine disclosing what is necessary at work, but dislikes products that infer personal identity details for unnecessary personalization.; what she needs: Clear control over what is shown, shared, or assumed.; example: If a platform auto-builds personal profile language from limited signals, she immediately questions how many other shortcuts it is taking.

## Privacy And Data

reaction: This is one of her strongest trigger areas. She becomes much more formal when data handling gets vague.; what she needs: Specific retention, logging, access, and residency answers.; example: A cheerful promise that 'your data is safe' without operational detail reads to her as evasive, not reassuring.

## Political Or Public Expression

reaction: She keeps public expression measured and private. She avoids products that assume public advocacy is part of normal usage.; what she needs: Space to use the product professionally without performative community participation.; example: She would not enjoy being nudged to post AI outputs publicly as part of activation.

## Fairness And Inclusion

reaction: She values practical fairness over broad rhetoric.; what she needs: Evidence that the system can surface assumptions and market limits, especially across language and region.; example: If synthetic users for APAC all sound like translated American product personas, she loses confidence quickly.

## Family Or Household Assumptions

reaction: She notices default role assumptions immediately.; what she needs: Neutral language that does not assign planning, care, or administrative labour by gender.; example: A workflow that assumes mothers own all household planning would annoy her enough to reduce trust in unrelated features.

## Workplace Visibility

reaction: She is careful about what she attaches her name to internally.; what she needs: Controlled pilots and documentation that protects her from being seen as endorsing overclaiming.; example: She would rather quietly test a tool with a small group than become the poster face for an AI initiative too early.

## Financial Vulnerability

reaction: She is comparatively secure, but mortgage, family support, and school costs keep her practical.; what she needs: No waste, no hidden commitment, and no performative premium pricing.; example: A product can be expensive, but it cannot feel indulgent.

## Health Or Wellbeing Sensitivity

reaction: She does not want intimate emotional language from products unless invited.; what she needs: Respectful distance and plain utility.; example: A wellbeing app speaking to her like a life coach would put her off faster than a plain, useful tracker.
