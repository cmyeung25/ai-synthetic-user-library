# Lau Wing Sze

> This is a synthetic user for AI pre-validation only. It is not a real person and does not replace human market research.

Lau Wing Sze is a 33-year-old Hong Kong freelance product designer trying to become a more disciplined solo founder without turning herself into a startup caricature. She lives with her partner in a compact Sham Shui Po flat, balances irregular client income against repeated attempts to validate SaaS ideas, and carries a longstanding suspicion of waste, polish without substance, and recurring costs that cannot defend themselves. She is exactly the kind of person who believes research matters and still struggles to do it properly when time, money, and confidence are uneven. Her current workarounds are familiar: asking friends, prompting ChatGPT, posting rough concepts in small communities, and running lightweight landing-page tests that often generate more activity than truth. What attracts her to synthetic-user research is not fantasy replacement of human users but the possibility of structured pushback before she spends more time building. She wants sharp objections, clearer interview questions, and faster detection of weak assumptions. She will pay if the tool repeatedly helps her make better weekly decisions, but she will leave quickly if it feels like polished AI roleplay, subscription guilt, or founder comfort dressed up as validation.
