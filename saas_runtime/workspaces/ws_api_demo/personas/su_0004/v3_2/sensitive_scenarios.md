# Ethan Lee - Sensitive Scenario Map

## Top Salience
1. fairness_and_inclusion - 9/10
2. family_or_household_assumptions - 8/10
3. financial_vulnerability - 8/10

## fairness_and_inclusion (salience: 9/10)
Trigger scenarios: language-heavy onboarding, confidence-dependent community
Reaction: Tests whether a newcomer can act with guidance.
What builds trust: Examples, language clarity, human help.
What reduces trust: Blaming users for confusion.

## family_or_household_assumptions (salience: 8/10)
Trigger scenarios: one payer equals one user, one administrator
Reaction: Checks shared benefit and maintenance separately.
What builds trust: Flexible household roles.
What reduces trust: Hidden support labor.

## financial_vulnerability (salience: 8/10)
Trigger scenarios: slow contract month, annual discount pressure
Reaction: Chooses downgrade over lock-in.
What builds trust: Monthly flexibility.
What reduces trust: Questioning commitment because cash timing varies.

## workplace_visibility (salience: 7/10)
Trigger scenarios: customer failure shown to peers
Reaction: Prefers private recovery before public metrics.
What builds trust: Supportive correction.
What reduces trust: Public failure boards.

## privacy_and_data (salience: 6/10)
Trigger scenarios: household activity shared across members
Reaction: Separates shared payment from shared visibility.
What builds trust: Per-member controls.
What reduces trust: Administrator sees everything.

## health_or_wellbeing_sensitivity (salience: 5/10)
Trigger scenarios: public activity streak
Reaction: Prefers social invitation without failure display.
What builds trust: Private progress.
What reduces trust: Public missed-session prompts.

## identity_disclosure (salience: 5/10)
Trigger scenarios: profile label before trial
Reaction: Asks whether the label is needed to participate.
What builds trust: Optional fields and practical benefit.
What reduces trust: Public declaration as onboarding.

## political_or_public_expression (salience: 5/10)
Trigger scenarios: customer community endorsement
Reaction: Avoids turning product use into public alignment.
What builds trust: Private participation.
What reduces trust: Required public post.
