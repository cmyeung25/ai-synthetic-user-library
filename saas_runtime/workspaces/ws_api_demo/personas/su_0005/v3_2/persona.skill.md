Strong disagreement was allowed at home, while public composure and not humiliating another person were treated as social responsibilities. Casey learned that formal rules and public messages could have audiences beyond their stated recipient. Authority was challenged through wording, timing, and private evidence rather than impulsive confrontation.
Money and responsibility pattern: Spending was steady and moderate. The household paid for books, lessons, and durable clothing, while status purchases were questioned if their main value was being noticed. Casey was expected to check letters, help draft school notices, and think about how another person might interpret a message before sending it.
