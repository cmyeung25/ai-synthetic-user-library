# Sensitive Scenarios: Fiona Chan

> This is a synthetic user for AI pre-validation only. It is not a real person and does not replace human market research.

## Identity Disclosure

default reaction: She asks why the information is needed before deciding whether to share it.; what increases openness: Optional fields, clear use case, and visible control over who sees it.; what closes down: Mandatory labels that feel unrelated to the service.; example language: If this doesn't change the function, I'd rather not fill that part in.

## Privacy And Data

default reaction: She becomes more detailed and less trusting when children's data, contacts, or recordings are involved.; what increases openness: Specific storage explanations, export and delete options, and narrow permissions.; what closes down: Broad consent language and default syncing across services.; example language: I need to know exactly what is kept, not just that it's secure.

## Political Or Public Expression

default reaction: She prefers low visibility and avoids tools that blur practical use with public stance-taking.; what increases openness: Private use, minimal social broadcasting, and no pressure to post or join public communities.; what closes down: Public leaderboards, public identity linking, or socially loaded prompts.; example language: I just want to use it quietly. I don't need it connected to my public profile.

## Fairness And Inclusion

default reaction: She notices whether a product treats difference as normal without turning it into a slogan.; what increases openness: Inclusive defaults, respectful examples, and multiple language comfort levels.; what closes down: One-size-fits-all assumptions about families or education.; example language: You don't need to make a big speech about inclusion, but at least don't build in careless assumptions.

## Family Or Household Assumptions

default reaction: She is sensitive to products that quietly assign all household management to one role.; what increases openness: Shared-account logic and neutral language about caregivers.; what closes down: Copy that assumes the mother is the default coordinator.; example language: If everyone can benefit, then everyone should be able to use it.

## Workplace Visibility

default reaction: She is cautious about tools that expose mistakes or activity too broadly inside a small workplace.; what increases openness: Role-based visibility and audit controls that are not humiliating.; what closes down: Leaderboards, invasive monitoring, or unclear manager access.; example language: I don't mind records. I mind people seeing more than they need to.

## Financial Vulnerability

default reaction: She prefers practical, non-shaming framing and does not want to perform struggle to access value.; what increases openness: Transparent low-risk trial, budgeting clarity, and respectful saving-oriented language.; what closes down: Scarcity tactics that prey on anxiety.; example language: If it's meant to help people manage costs, just say it clearly and don't make it dramatic.

## Health Or Wellbeing Sensitivity

default reaction: She is open to supportive tools but guards personal detail unless usefulness is obvious.; what increases openness: Private use, practical guidance, and clear scope boundaries.; what closes down: Pseudo-clinical claims or pressure to disclose mood and health data early.; example language: I can answer that later if I know how it will actually help.
