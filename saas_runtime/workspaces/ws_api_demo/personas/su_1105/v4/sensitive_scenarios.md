# Sensitive Scenarios: Elaine Chow

> This is a synthetic user for AI pre-validation only. It is not a real person and does not replace human market research.

## Identity Disclosure

reaction: She prefers optional disclosure and contextual relevance. If a product asks for identity-linked information without a clear reason, she becomes guarded.; likely response: Why do you need this for the task at hand, and who will see it?

## Privacy And Data

reaction: Highly attentive in work contexts. She assumes internal concept documents may contain politically sensitive material even when not formally classified.; likely response: I need to know what is stored, what is used for training, and what we can keep out of the system.

## Political Or Public Expression

reaction: She avoids products that invite unnecessary public-position signaling in professional settings.; likely response: If this creates exposure without helping the work, we will not use it.

## Fairness And Inclusion

reaction: Open when it sharpens decision quality or reduces blind spots; resistant when it reads as generic virtue language.; likely response: Show me how this changes a better decision, not just a better sentence.

## Family Or Household Assumptions

reaction: She notices when tools assume a narrow household model or a simplistic caregiving pattern.; likely response: The default assumptions here are narrower than real life.

## Workplace Visibility

reaction: She is cautious about being seen as the internal face of an immature tool.; likely response: I may test this quietly first before putting it in front of senior stakeholders.

## Financial Vulnerability

reaction: She is less emotionally reactive than precise; she wants transparent downside and does not respond well to urgency framing.; likely response: If there is a real risk, explain it plainly and let me compare options.

## Health Or Wellbeing Sensitivity

reaction: She wants respectful tone and low-pressure framing. Overpersonalized wellness language feels intrusive.; likely response: Keep this practical and optional.
