# Interview Methods

## Technique Selection

Use the transcript to choose one technique at a time.

| Signal | Technique | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| General opinion without an event | Critical incident | "Tell me about the last time that happened." |
| Missing sequence or handoff | Journey reconstruction | "What happened immediately before and after that?" |
| Stated reason may be superficial | Adaptive Five Whys | "What made that consequence matter in that situation?" |
| Value or identity is implicit | Laddering | "What would that allow you to protect or achieve?" |
| Cause may be overgeneralized | Counterexample | "When does this process work well enough?" |
| Claimed need may not change action | Counterfactual | "If that constraint disappeared tomorrow, what would you do differently?" |
| Polite or hypothetical interest | Commitment evidence | "What have you already tried, paid for, or changed?" |
| Sensitive issue appears | Permission and relevance check | "Is it useful to explore that, or should we stay with the practical workflow?" |

## Evidence Strength

- Stronger: recent specific event, observable action, named workaround, actual cost, repeated pattern, failed attempt.
- Moderate: detailed explanation consistent with more than one event.
- Weaker: generic preference, prediction, idealized self-description, agreement with interviewer language.

## Root-Cause Hypotheses

A root-cause hypothesis must include supporting transcript references, at least one alternative explanation, confidence, and a validation gap. Phrase it as a testable explanation, not a fact.

## Synthesis Outputs

- Insight: evidence-backed tension or pattern, not a transcript summary.
- Need: solution-neutral desired progress.
- POV: `[participant context] needs [need] because [evidence-backed insight]`.
- HMW: broad enough for alternatives, narrow enough to test, and free of an embedded solution.
