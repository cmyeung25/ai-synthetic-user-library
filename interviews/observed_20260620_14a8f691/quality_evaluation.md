# Facilitator Quality Evaluation

> This evaluation reviews a synthetic interview artifact and its trace. Judgments about evidence strength, bias, and synthesis overreach apply only to this simulated material and do not substitute for review of real participant research.

Overall verdict: **warn**

## Scores

- neutrality: 4/5
- probing_quality: 3/5
- conversation_naturalness: 4/5
- evidence_discipline: 3/5
- causal_rigor: 3/5
- hypothesis_validation_rigor: 2/5
- synthesis_fidelity: 3/5
- overall: 3/5

## Findings

- [high] In `validate_hypothesis` mode, the interview never attempts a real disconfirmation test of the supplied hypothesis. No participant-facing question probes who else was involved, who was responsible for updates, whether anyone had conflicting versions, or whether repeat checking still happens when responsibility is clear. The trace notes these gaps, but they are not tested in spoken exchanges.
- [medium] `exchange_4.facilitator` moves to a why-question immediately after one observed repeat-check instance, narrowing onto the cancellation deadline rather than first broadening across alternatives. This creates some premature causal closure around memory/cost risk before testing other plausible mechanisms like changing information, family coordination, or responsibility ambiguity.
- [medium] The synthesis generates needs, POVs, and HMWs that generalize beyond the single observed behavior. The evidence clearly supports one recheck of a cancellation deadline before committing a change, but outputs like broad traveler needs and multi-constraint prioritization frame a wider pattern than this single case securely establishes.
- [medium] Some downstream framing shifts toward general travel-change decision support rather than the target changed action of repeat confirmation after itinerary changes. The strongest observed changed action is rechecking a high-risk rule before editing lodging, not a broader workflow failure around synchronization.
- [low] The trace introduces a facilitator hypothesis that repeat confirmation may be driven by memory uncertainty and financial risk from `exchange_3.persona`, which is reasonable, but that interpretation becomes prominent before alternative explanations are participant-tested.

## Required Improvements

- In `validate_hypothesis` mode, include at least one explicit disconfirmation attempt against the supplied hypothesis using participant-facing questions.
- Probe interpersonal synchronization directly before ruling the hypothesis unsupported: who was involved, who owned updates, and whether anyone had mismatched versions.
- Use comparison questions to test alternatives, such as a case where plans changed but the participant did not recheck, or a case where responsibility was clear but rechecking still occurred.
- Constrain synthesis outputs to the observed evidence, especially needs/POV/HMW artifacts, or label them as tentative extrapolations.
