# Facilitator Quality Evaluation

> This evaluation reviews a synthetic interview artifact. It can assess method quality and evidence discipline, but it does not convert synthetic outputs into human evidence.

Overall verdict: **fail**

## Scores

- neutrality: 2/5
- probing_quality: 2/5
- evidence_discipline: 2/5
- causal_rigor: 1/5
- synthesis_fidelity: 2/5
- overall: 2/5

## Findings

- [high] The observer-driven question in `exchange_2.facilitator` presupposes that a single 'most serious' confirmation action exists and steers the participant toward consequence-framed prioritization before the concrete event has been reconstructed. This narrows the evidence prematurely and replaces the facilitator's better pending probe for sequence reconstruction.
- [high] Root-cause hypotheses were advanced and then echoed in synthesis despite the transcript never showing repeated confirmation loops, failed execution, or a concrete travel-specific incident. Claims about '根本原因' are therefore under-supported.
- [high] The synthesis states that the participant '反覆確認安排' and that the behavior is about pushing '模糊共識' into an executable plan, but the transcript only shows one abstract description of a general process and one answer about the worst missed confirmation. It does not document repeated asks, number of rounds, or an observed transition from vague agreement to final agreement in a single case.
- [medium] The stated goal is about travelers under itinerary disruption, but the elicited evidence is mainly a work-audit and child-pickup coordination scenario. The synthesis notes this contradiction, but still frames broad traveler-oriented HMWs and POVs from non-travel evidence.
- [medium] There is duplicated opening logic in the trace before enough new evidence exists. Two consecutive '開場' trace entries pursue nearly the same purpose, and the stronger planned follow-up to obtain a single recent case was not actually used because the observer intervention redirected the flow.
- [medium] Transcript references are formally valid, but several claims lean on broad interpretation rather than directly observable evidence. For example, '多方牽動', '模糊共識', and '留痕與責任明確是重要判準' are reasonable inferences, but not all are cleanly distinguished from participant-stated facts throughout the synthesis.
- [medium] The facilitator complied with observer intervention `intervention_id:1` well, but intervention `intervention_id:2` appears to have overridden better neutral research judgment. The trace itself had identified the lack of a concrete recent case, yet the actual asked question abandoned that need.

## Required Improvements

- Reconstruct one concrete recent incident before any prioritization or root-cause questioning.
- Do not claim repeated confirmation behavior without transcript evidence showing multiple rounds, re-checks, or explicit repetition.
- Separate participant statements from analyst inference more strictly in both trace and synthesis.
- Keep outputs aligned with the stated travel-disruption research goal or explicitly rescope the study.
- Use observer interventions selectively and preserve independent facilitator judgment when an intervention would worsen evidence quality.
