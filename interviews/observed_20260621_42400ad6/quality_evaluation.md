# Facilitator Quality Evaluation

> This audit evaluates a synthetic interview artifact, not a real participant session. Findings address methodological quality and evidence discipline within the simulation and should not be treated as market evidence.

Overall verdict: **warn**

## Scores

- neutrality: 3/5
- probing_quality: 3/5
- conversation_naturalness: 4/5
- evidence_discipline: 2/5
- causal_rigor: 3/5
- hypothesis_validation_rigor: 3/5
- synthesis_fidelity: 2/5
- overall: 3/5

## Findings

- [medium] The concept is introduced with a bundled value proposition that presumes benefit: '一次過睇附近活動、篩時間地點價錢、直接訂同付款'. This is more than neutral exposure and invites feature selection from researcher-supplied benefits rather than an open reaction to a minimally described concept.
- [medium] Several later questions are still useful but become somewhat questionnaire-like after concept introduction, moving feature-by-feature through trust, sharing, retention, recommendations, pricing, and founder assumptions without adapting based on the strongest observed issue: coordination and payment risk in the recent event.
- [high] The synthesis upgrades hypothetical concept reactions into supported assumption claims. For example, '即時名額／即時確認會增加信任' is marked `partially_supported`, but the cited evidence only shows hypothetical desire for faster coordination and direct booking, not observed trust effects from real-time inventory/confirmation. Likewise, pricing, retention, recommendation, and sharing conclusions rely heavily on hypothetical answers rather than observed behavior.
- [high] The report overstates concept validation breadth for a single synthetic interview. Items such as `workflow_effect: replaces_workflow`, multiple `supported` assumption validations, and a concrete product experiment direction go beyond what one simulated participant can justify, especially when much of the evidence is concept-response rather than observed adoption behavior.
- [medium] The interview does identify changed action bottlenecks in group coordination, but the synthesis still treats several adjacent feature areas as validated domain requirements. Recommendation logic, service-fee acceptance, and retention conditions were not observed as changed actions; they were elicited as forecasts about a proposed product.
- [medium] Most references point to the right exchange, but several synthesis claims are broader than their cited material. Example: `save 或 share 容易造成假陽性興趣` is marked `supported`, yet `exchange_1.persona` does not discuss save/share false positives directly; it discusses waiting for replies and payment hesitation. The conclusion is plausible but not exact-semantic support.

## Required Improvements

- Relabel hypothetical concept reactions as hypothetical or stated preferences throughout the synthesis instead of treating them as supported behavioral evidence.
- Reduce concept selling in the introduction question and ask one open process-change question before feature-specific probes.
- Keep domain conclusions bounded to observed actions in the recent event: discovery source, comparison behavior, coordination delay, and payment hesitation.
- Avoid broad product outputs such as workflow replacement, retention confidence, or validated assumption sets from a single synthetic concept interview.
- Strengthen transcript-to-claim discipline by ensuring each strong synthesis statement has an exact matching participant quote or narrowing the claim.
