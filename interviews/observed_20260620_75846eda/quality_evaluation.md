# Facilitator Quality Evaluation

> This audit evaluates only the supplied synthetic interview materials. Judgments about interview quality and evidence strength should not be treated as findings about real users without human review and real participant data.

Overall verdict: **warn**

## Scores

- neutrality: 3/5
- probing_quality: 3/5
- conversation_naturalness: 3/5
- evidence_discipline: 4/5
- causal_rigor: 4/5
- synthesis_fidelity: 3/5
- overall: 4/5

## Findings

- [medium] The heard exchange only partially matches the research domain. The participant's story is primarily about a school schedule change cascading into transport changes, and the facilitator needed a second question just to establish that travel itself was not the main disrupted domain.
- [medium] The second asked question is contrastive and somewhat narrowing: it offers two frames ('旅行出發被打亂' versus '學校通知變動才臨時重排交通') instead of inviting the participant to characterize the situation in their own words.
- [medium] Some synthesis items convert adjacent evidence into generalized traveler needs and POV statements. Claims about '需要銜接多段安排的人' and '旅客' go beyond what this transcript directly supports because the participant explicitly said the trip itself was not the problem.
- [low] The root-cause hypotheses are appropriately low-confidence and list alternatives, but they still lean into causal framing from a single off-target incident with very limited behavioral sequence detail.
- [low] The exchange is reasonably conversational, but the facilitator's second question sounds analytical rather than naturally curious because it performs classification mid-conversation.

## Required Improvements

- Add an earlier domain-screening step so adjacent school/work/family coordination stories do not enter the main evidence set as travel interviews.
- Rewrite fit-check probes to avoid forced either-or framing and let the participant describe the trigger and impact sequence first.
- Do not express adjacent evidence as traveler 'needs' or POV statements unless clearly marked as unvalidated hypotheses derived from a non-matching case.
- Delay root-cause synthesis until an in-domain case includes observed confirmation sequence, consequences, and at least one alternative or contrast case.
