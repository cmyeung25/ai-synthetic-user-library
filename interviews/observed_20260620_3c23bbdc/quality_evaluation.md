# Facilitator Quality Evaluation

> This audit evaluates a synthetic interview artifact and its synthesis quality, not real participant evidence; any conclusions should be treated as method feedback only.

Overall verdict: **warn**

## Scores

- neutrality: 3/5
- probing_quality: 3/5
- conversation_naturalness: 3/5
- evidence_discipline: 4/5
- causal_rigor: 3/5
- synthesis_fidelity: 3/5
- overall: 4/5

## Findings

- [medium] The trace introduces root-cause hypotheses about loss-risk prioritization and family schedule risk before direct evidence of repeated confirmation exists. These are framed as low confidence, but they still steer the inquiry toward causal explanations before the core behavior in the research goal is established.
- [medium] Several needs, POV statements, and HMWs are built from adjacent evidence about schedule coordination, refund deadlines, and feasibility, not from observed repeated-confirmation behavior. They are plausible for travel replanning, but they do not cleanly answer the stated goal about the root cause of repeated confirmation.
- [medium] The discussed experience is a real travel-arrangement change, but much of the evidence centers on family schedule constraints triggered partly by a school notice. The synthesis correctly keeps it in scope, yet some outputs drift toward general household coordination rather than showing that target-domain travel actions changed because of repeated confirmation behavior.
- [low] The interview reads reasonably natural, but the facilitator’s third question is slightly packed, repeating two decision variables in one sentence. The persona also answers in a highly tidy, fully reasoned way that sounds somewhat more synthesized than spontaneous.
- [low] Observer intervention 2 suggested a forced severity-ranking question about which missed confirmation would have the worst consequence. The facilitator did well not to ask it, but the planning record still shows pressure toward hypothetical consequence framing before confirming whether any repeated check occurred.
- [low] The persona response structure is coherent and useful, but it is unusually complete and analytically organized for a short live answer, especially in exchange 3 where criteria, coordination, and back-planning are all neatly summarized.

## Required Improvements

- Establish the existence, object, and timing of repeated confirmation before generating root-cause hypotheses tied to the research goal.
- Do not derive target-domain needs, POVs, or HMWs for repeated-confirmation causes from adjacent-only evidence about general travel replanning.
- Keep participant-facing probes single-focus and avoid hypothetical fit-check or consequence-ranking questions until actual behavior is observed.
