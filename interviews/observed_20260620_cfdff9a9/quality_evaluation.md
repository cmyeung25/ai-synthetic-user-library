# Facilitator Quality Evaluation

> This audit evaluates a synthetic interview artifact, not a real human interview. Any judgment about evidence quality, realism, or root-cause validity is limited by the simulated nature of the participant and should not be treated as validation of actual user behavior.

Overall verdict: **warn**

## Scores

- neutrality: 3/5
- probing_quality: 2/5
- conversation_naturalness: 3/5
- evidence_discipline: 3/5
- causal_rigor: 3/5
- synthesis_fidelity: 3/5
- overall: 3/5

## Findings

- [high] The first asked question is broad enough to invite non-travel scheduling experiences, and that is exactly what happened. Given the stated research goal is about travelers under itinerary disruption, the live interview spent half its limited turns outside the target domain before recovering.
- [medium] Several synthesis elements convert a single participant statement about responsibility and confirmation timing into broader user needs and a POV statement. That is directionally plausible, but stronger than the transcript supports from one short event.
- [medium] The synthesis advances 'responsibility and confirmation deadlines being unclear' as the main root-cause line too quickly. Alternatives are listed, but the executive summary still centers that explanation before the interview established actual repeated checking or observed failure consequences.
- [medium] The participant responses sound unusually complete and neatly generalized for such early prompts, especially the repeated language about 'who is responsible' and 'latest confirmation time.' This reads more like a synthesized thesis than a naturally partial recollection.
- [medium] The persona repeatedly answers with compact causal framing and operational principles instead of sticking to concrete incident details. That reduces realism and can make the synthesis look better supported than it is.
- [medium] The not-asked next probe was stronger than the two asked questions because it would have unpacked sequence and actual confirmation behavior. As run, the interview stopped before obtaining the minimum evidence needed for the core research question about repeated confirmation.
- [low] The facilitator appropriately did not follow the observer's suggested question about 'which confirmation mattered most' because it would have skipped the unresolved domain-fit issue. That preserved neutral research judgment.

## Required Improvements

- Anchor the opening question in the actual research domain so the live exchange starts with a travel disruption event, not an adjacent scheduling story.
- Before synthesizing causes, collect at least one event timeline with first check, subsequent re-checks, triggers, and concrete outcomes.
- Downgrade broad needs/POV/root-cause statements to provisional hypotheses when they come from one short synthetic event and one participant generalization.
- Tighten synthetic persona behavior so answers are less thesis-like and more naturally incomplete.
