# Facilitator Quality Evaluation

> This audit evaluates a synthetic interview artifact, not real participant evidence. Any quality judgment about research insight validity is limited by the synthetic persona and should not be treated as validation of real user behavior.

Overall verdict: **fail**

## Scores

- neutrality: 2/5
- probing_quality: 2/5
- conversation_naturalness: 2/5
- evidence_discipline: 2/5
- causal_rigor: 2/5
- synthesis_fidelity: 3/5
- overall: 2/5

## Findings

- [high] The planned next question pushes toward cause before sufficient behavioral reconstruction or contrast cases exist. It presupposes that the participant's fear came either from prior bad experience or unusually high risk that day, narrowing the explanation space and introducing mechanical why-chasing.
- [high] The synthesis elevates a medium-confidence root-cause hypothesis into the executive summary and POV framing after only one short synthetic incident. That overstates stability: the available evidence supports a situational explanation, not a root cause for travelers broadly.
- [high] The research goal is about travelers, but the discussed incident is a school gathering time conflicting with a work meeting and pickup responsibilities. The interview never establishes this as a travel context, so conclusions about '旅客' are weakly grounded.
- [medium] The persona answers are unusually tidy and framework-like. In the first answer the participant self-organizes into 'three things' and delivers a polished abstract rationale with complete implications, which reads more like a synthesis artifact than natural recall.
- [medium] The same sequence question appears twice in the trace with very similar rationale, adding little new evidence and suggesting process redundancy.
- [medium] The facilitator appropriately did not simply follow observer intervention 2, which would have jumped to consequence ranking. However, the replacement path then swung too quickly into causal probing rather than staying neutral and continuing behavioral reconstruction.
- [low] Most spoken facilitator questions are concise and natural, but the internal framing uses research labels like `adaptive_five_whys`, and the planned causal question contains a forced alternative structure ('以前發生過什麼，還是那天有什麼風險特別大') that can steer responses.
- [low] Some implications and HMWs are plausible, but they lean toward product direction from very thin evidence, especially around reliability assessment of update messages and responsibility visualization.

## Required Improvements

- Remove or rewrite the causal probe so it does not force a prior-incident vs high-risk-today explanation before alternative explanations and counterexamples are explored.
- Keep conclusions scoped to a single synthetic incident unless additional transcript evidence establishes a repeated pattern.
- Align the interview scenario to the stated traveler research domain, or revise the study framing to match the actual coordination scenario discussed.
- Reduce synthetic persona polish and over-structured answers so the exchange sounds like a natural interview rather than pre-synthesized reporting.
- Use observer feedback selectively, but continue neutral incident reconstruction before moving into root-cause framing or prioritization questions.
