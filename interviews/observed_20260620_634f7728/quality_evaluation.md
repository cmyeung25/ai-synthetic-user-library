# Facilitator Quality Evaluation

> This audit evaluates a single synthetic interview and its artifacts. Judgments about causal strength, hypothesis status, and synthesis quality should not be treated as evidence about real travelers without human interview validation.

Overall verdict: **warn**

## Scores

- neutrality: 4/5
- probing_quality: 4/5
- conversation_naturalness: 4/5
- evidence_discipline: 4/5
- causal_rigor: 3/5
- hypothesis_validation_rigor: 3/5
- synthesis_fidelity: 4/5
- overall: 4/5

## Findings

- [medium] The trace shows an appropriate participant-led causal probe was drafted (`"那晚你會再看一次，最主要是卡在哪一點？"`) but then rejected, and the live interview moved to mechanism testing first. That leaves the validation weaker because the participant's own open causal account was never directly elicited before testing researcher-supplied mechanisms.
- [medium] The interview disconfirms the main hypothesis with a condition-present versus condition-absent contrast, but it does not isolate the promoted alternative explanations. For example, 'memory aid' and 'system transparency' are advanced in synthesis without their own participant-facing probes that vary or isolate those conditions.
- [medium] Some alternative tests are overstated as 'tested'. The explanation '改動生效不確定性導致反覆確認' is consistent with the transcript, but no participant-facing question isolated that condition against a contrasting case where update certainty was high; similarly, the 'memory burden' explanation comes from one volunteered comment and was not directly tested.
- [low] `exchange_5.facilitator` is slightly compound because it combines a near-miss probe with a normal-outcome alternative in one sentence. It remains understandable, but it splits the participant's focus between two response paths.

## Required Improvements

- Ask one open participant-led causal question immediately after the repeated-checking behavior is observed, before mechanism-specific probes.
- Do not present alternative explanations as tested unless a participant-facing probe isolates or contrasts that explanation.
- Keep consequence probes to one conversational focus per spoken question when possible.
- Maintain explicit wording in synthesis that distinguishes observed disconfirmation of the supplied hypothesis from merely plausible alternative explanations.
