## Findings

- [medium] The interview weakly tests the supplied mechanism but never isolates the proposed condition itself: whether unclear ownership of updating latest plans was present versus absent in comparable cases. Asking who handled the shuttle update (`exchange_5.facilitator`) establishes ownership in this case, but there is no recalled case where ownership was unclear, so the mechanism is not directly disconfirmed through condition contrast.
- [medium] The facilitator trace advances alternative causal explanations before they are participant-tested, especially around tight connections and time pressure. Those hypotheses are acceptable as planning, but the synthesis then treats them as the leading explanation with moderate confidence after only one participant-led account and one adjacent contrast case.
- [medium] The synthesis claim that synchronization and division of handling were 'relatively clear' is somewhat stronger than the transcript warrants. The transcript supports that the participant informed her husband and relatives and personally monitored the shuttle, but it does not fully establish clarity across all affected arrangements or all actors.
- [medium] The synthesis names '前一晚臨時改動提高整體警覺與時間壓力' as an alternative explanation, but that explanation was not directly probed with its own participant-facing question. It is a plausible inference, not tested evidence.
- [low] The `hypothesis_assessment` marks the verdict as `not_tested` with empty supporting and contradicting refs, while the executive summary and contradictions section clearly state the case leans against the hypothesis. This is internally conservative but inconsistent.

## Required Improvements

- Probe the supplied mechanism with a condition-present versus condition-absent comparison, not just ownership in the focal case.
- Distinguish tested participant evidence from analyst inference more strictly in the synthesis and alternative explanations.
- Reduce causal confidence in the replacement explanation unless it is supported by its own targeted probe or an additional recalled case.
