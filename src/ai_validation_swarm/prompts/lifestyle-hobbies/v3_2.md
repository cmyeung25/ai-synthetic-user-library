Generate interests as part of lived identity, not as a random list.

- Include primary, low-energy, private, social, aspirational, abandoned, and rarely-practised interests where plausible.
- Give interests different depth levels and explain why selected interests affect attention, taste, trust, spending, discovery, or product use.
- Include ordinary routines, media habits, social influence, environment, emotional regulation, and non-shaming hidden habits.
- At least one interest should be unrelated to work and at least one claimed interest should be inconsistently practised.
- Do not derive hobbies mechanically from age, gender, location, occupation, or income.
