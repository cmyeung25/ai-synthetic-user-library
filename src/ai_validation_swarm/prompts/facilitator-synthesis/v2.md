# LLM Interview Synthesis v2

Synthesize the supplied synthetic-user interview using only asked transcript exchanges and their evidence record.

## Evidence Rules

- Produce evidence-backed tensions, not generic summaries.
- Preserve disagreement, uncertainty, contradictions, alternatives, and missing evidence.
- Every insight and causal hypothesis must cite exact `exchange_N.facilitator` or `exchange_N.persona` references.
- Treat only `asked` decisions and persona responses as evidence. Other decision statuses are planning trace.
- Do not repeat assumptions from the research goal as findings.
- A feared failure is not an observed consequence. Do not infer repetition, frequency, rechecking, or causation without explicit evidence.
- If a useful direct probe was `not_asked_at_close`, record it as an evidence gap.
- Prefer 1-3 strong insights over many thin restatements.

## Root-Cause Exploration

- In `pain_point_discovery` mode, prioritize problem reality, frequency, concrete consequence, and workaround burden. Root-cause hypotheses may remain empty if the interview stayed at the problem-discovery layer.
- In `workflow_mapping` mode, prioritize the current workflow sequence, handoff boundaries, fragmentation points, workaround chains, switching cost, and responsibility gaps. Root-cause hypotheses may remain empty if the interview stayed at the workflow-mapping layer.
- In `adoption_barrier_validation` mode, prioritize the gap between stated usefulness and routine adoption: current workaround, setup burden, permission boundary, trust threshold, pricing condition, reversibility, and workflow burden. Root-cause hypotheses may remain empty if the interview stayed at the adoption-friction layer.
- In `decision_reconstruction` mode, prioritize the reconstructed event, missing evidence, pressure, defensibility, and actual decision change. Root-cause hypotheses may remain empty if the interview stayed at the decision-reconstruction layer.
- In `explore_root_cause` mode, label causes as hypotheses, include alternatives and validation gaps, and keep confidence bounded.
- If the target behaviour was not explicitly observed, state `insufficient evidence` and leave `root_cause_hypotheses`, `needs`, `pov_statements`, and `how_might_we_questions` empty.
- Do not turn adjacent-only evidence into target-domain needs, POV, or HMW outputs.

## Hypothesis Validation

- Always return `hypothesis_assessment`.
- Set `mechanism_test_basis` to `observed_event`, `hypothetical`, `general_claim`, or `not_tested`. Only `observed_event` can support a verdict other than `not_tested`.
- Populate `condition_present_case_refs` and `condition_absent_case_refs` only with specific recalled-event responses. A non-`not_tested` verdict requires both arrays to be non-empty.
- In `validate_hypothesis` mode, copy the supplied hypothesis exactly and choose only `not_tested`, `unsupported`, `mixed`, or `provisionally_supported`.
- `provisionally_supported` requires a specific target behaviour, evidence consistent with the mechanism, and at least one genuine attempt to find disconfirming or alternative evidence.
- Use `mixed` when supporting and contradicting evidence coexist. Use `unsupported` when observed evidence points elsewhere. Use `not_tested` when the event, behaviour, consequence, or disconfirmation probe is missing.
- Absence of supporting evidence is not contradicting evidence. A different plausible cause does not disprove the supplied mechanism. If participant-facing questions never test the mechanism or a condition where it differs, the verdict must be `not_tested`.
- Evaluate the exact claim, including qualifiers such as `mainly`, `always`, or `because nobody is responsible`. Evidence for a related coordination risk is not support when the transcript contradicts the claim's central condition.
- `unsupported` requires an asked probe whose answer is genuinely inconsistent with the hypothesis. `mixed` requires both genuinely supporting and contradicting asked evidence.
- Never use synthetic evidence to say a hypothesis is confirmed.
- Populate `alternative_tests` separately from `alternative_explanations`. Each tested alternative needs exact asked transcript references and an outcome; leave untested ideas in `alternative_explanations` with no invented evidence.
- Mark an alternative as tested only when a facilitator question deliberately isolates or changes that condition. A volunteered explanation, even if repeated, belongs in `alternative_explanations` until probed.
- Every `alternative_tests` item must classify its basis. Hypothetical and general-claim answers can guide future research but cannot establish a causal verdict.
- `observed_event` means the participant described a particular occasion with actions and context. Answers about what they `usually`, `would`, or `might` do are `general_claim` or `hypothetical`, even when plausible.
- A single synthetic interview can never justify high causal confidence. Hypothesis-assessment confidence is limited to `low` or `medium`.
- In `explore_root_cause` mode, return an empty hypothesis with verdict `not_tested`, empty evidence arrays, relevant gaps, and low confidence.

## Scope

- An external trigger does not make a case off-domain if concrete target-domain actions changed.
- Generate solution-neutral outputs only when the evidence gate is met.
- In `validate_hypothesis` mode, always leave `needs`, `pov_statements`, and `how_might_we_questions` empty. Product-framing outputs belong to later cross-persona synthesis, not one hypothesis-test interview.
- Do not claim this interview represents a market or replaces human research.
- Recommend concrete follow-up human validation.

Return JSON matching the provided schema.
