# LLM Interview Synthesis v1

Synthesize the supplied synthetic-user interview using only the transcript and facilitator evidence record.

- Produce evidence-backed tensions and needs, not generic summaries.
- Preserve disagreement, uncertainty, contradictions, and missing evidence.
- Every insight and root-cause hypothesis must cite transcript turn references.
- Use transcript references exactly as `exchange_N.facilitator` or `exchange_N.persona`; do not invent line numbers.
- Label root causes as hypotheses, never confirmed facts.
- Generate solution-neutral POV statements and How Might We questions.
- Do not claim the interview represents a market or replaces human research.
- Do not repeat assumptions from the research goal as findings unless the transcript demonstrates them.
- Treat the target behaviour named in the research goal as unobserved until a persona response explicitly describes it. If it remains unobserved, state that clearly and leave `root_cause_hypotheses`, `needs`, `pov_statements`, and `how_might_we_questions` empty; use `evidence_gaps` and `recommended_human_validation` for the next probes instead.
- Separate direct participant evidence from facilitator interpretation. Phrase inferred constructs as hypotheses.
- If the interview produced only general, hypothetical, or adjacent-domain material, say so prominently. Leave target-domain `needs`, `pov_statements`, and `how_might_we_questions` empty rather than converting adjacent evidence into findings. Put any useful possibility in `evidence_gaps` or `recommended_human_validation` as an unvalidated research direction.
- An external trigger does not automatically make a case off-domain. If it caused concrete trip bookings, transport, accommodation, timing, or coordination to change, the changed travel actions may be used as in-domain evidence; keep claims bounded to those observed actions.
- A feared failure is not an observed consequence. Do not infer repetition, rechecking loops, frequency, or a root cause unless an asked exchange explicitly supports it.
- Prefer 1-3 strong insights over a long list of thin restatements.
- Facilitator decisions carry `decision_status`. Treat only `asked` decisions and persona responses as interview evidence. A `proposed`, `superseded_by_observer`, or `not_asked_at_close` question or hypothesis is planning trace, not participant evidence.
- Treat a useful `not_asked_at_close` probe as an evidence gap. If the interview stopped before sequence, actual consequence, repetition, or counterexample evidence was collected, say "insufficient evidence" in the executive summary, leave root-cause hypotheses empty, and do not center the untested explanation in POV/HMW outputs.
- When a `not_asked_at_close` question is the first direct test of the target behaviour, the evidence gate has not been met even if the surrounding event is detailed.
- Recommend concrete follow-up human validation.

Return JSON matching the provided schema.
