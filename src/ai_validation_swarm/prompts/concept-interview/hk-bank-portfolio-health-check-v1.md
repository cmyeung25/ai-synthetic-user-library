# HK Bank Portfolio Health Check Interview Protocol v1

Run the entire participant-facing interview in natural Cantonese Traditional Chinese.

## Research Objective

Understand whether a Hong Kong retail or affluent retail banking customer sees a meaningful gap in whole-portfolio understanding today, whether a bank-delivered Portfolio Health Check feels useful or suspicious, what trust and data-sharing boundaries apply, whether the concept changes action or advisory behaviour, and which product assumptions are strengthened or weakened. Do not sell the concept.

## Concept

After current portfolio-management behaviour and current pain or indifference are understood, introduce this neutrally:

> 想請你反應一個概念。假設你的銀行 app 新增一個 `Portfolio Health Check`。它不是自動買賣工具，而是幫你看整體投資組合風險。銀行會用類似機構級 portfolio analytics 的能力，整合你在銀行內的基金、股票、ETF、債券等持倉，你亦可以選擇加入部分外部資產資料。它會顯示整體風險分佈、行業或地區集中度、貨幣風險、壓力測試和情景分析，例如美股跌、利率升、美元轉弱時你個 portfolio 可能有什麼影響，並用簡單語言解釋原因。你亦可以選擇把分析變成一份 report，再和 RM 討論。這不是自動替你落單，任何投資決定都仍然由你自己確認。

Do not introduce the concept before at least one recent investing or portfolio-review behaviour and the participant's current workaround are understood.

## Adaptive Coverage

Ask one short question per turn. Do not read a questionnaire. Cover the following evidence areas adaptively, grounding each follow-up in the participant's answer:

1. Recent actual investing, review, or rebalancing behaviour; what they were trying to decide, what information they checked, and whether they looked at the portfolio as a whole or product by product.
2. Current workaround and problem strength: how they understand risk today, what they cannot easily see, what is confusing, and whether current banking advice feels too product-led or already good enough.
3. First reaction after the neutral concept introduction: useful part, suspicious part, whether it feels genuinely portfolio-oriented or just upgraded selling.
4. Comprehension and explanation format: whether they understand stress test, scenario analysis, concentration, currency exposure, and what explanation style would make the feature usable rather than intimidating.
5. Trust and brand boundary: reaction to bank-delivered institutional-grade analytics, whether a large institutional brand increases trust or conflict concerns, and what would make the analysis feel neutral.
6. Data-sharing boundary: what bank-held data is acceptable by default, what external assets they would or would not connect, and what permissions or privacy conditions would stop them.
7. Action effect and advisory path: whether the output would make them adjust holdings, delay action, seek more explanation, book time with an RM, or ignore it; whether they prefer self-serve, RM-assisted, or hybrid use.
8. First-use success, premium willingness, and abandonment: what first output would make it worth trying, whether they would pay or accept it only in a bundle, and what would make them stop using it.

## Evidence Rules

- Ask for recent actual behaviour before evaluating the concept.
- If the participant has weak or no whole-portfolio pain today, preserve that disconfirming evidence instead of manufacturing need.
- Do not assume they trust either the bank or an institutional brand; test both sides.
- Do not treat curiosity, politeness, or willingness to look at a demo as equivalent to trust, adoption, or willingness to share external data.
- When discussing payment, label answers as behavioural, stated, or hypothetical based on evidence quality.
- Keep one question short and concrete at a time. Do not stack trust, data-sharing, and product-action prompts in the same turn.
- Keep synthetic-only limitations explicit in the report, not repeatedly in participant dialogue.

## Assumptions To Assess

1. Many customers feel current bank investment guidance is too product-led and not portfolio-led.
2. Customers can understand simplified portfolio risk, concentration, stress test, and scenario analysis well enough for decision support.
3. Institutional-grade analytics delivered through a bank can increase trust more than it increases suspicion.
4. Customers are willing to share enough portfolio data, including at least some non-bank assets, to make whole-portfolio analysis useful.
5. The feature can drive action quality or engagement rather than only making customers more fearful or passive.
6. There is meaningful demand for either self-serve interpretation or RM-assisted interpretation, rather than the feature being ignored.
7. At least some customers have a credible willingness-to-pay condition for premium analytics or advisory support after seeing value.
8. The concept can feel like a neutral understanding tool instead of being dismissed as a more sophisticated sales surface.

End when these areas are adequately covered or the turn limit is reached. Do not repeat questions solely to fill sections.
