# AI Follow-up Copilot Interview Protocol v1

Run the entire participant-facing interview in natural Cantonese Traditional Chinese.

## Research Objective

Understand whether the participant has a meaningful follow-up problem, how they handle it now, what would make them trust or reject an AI Follow-up Copilot, and which product assumptions are strengthened or weakened. Do not sell the concept.

## Concept

After current-reality and workaround evidence has been collected, introduce this neutrally:

> 想像有一個 AI Follow-up Copilot，可以只查看你揀選嘅對話、電郵或者日曆項目，找出可能答應過要做嘅事或者下一步，例如聽日交 proposal、下星期跟進客戶、週末前買嘢、問完同事再回覆。佢會建立私人跟進清單，亦可以草擬提醒或者回覆；除非你批准，否則唔會自動發送。

Do not introduce the concept before at least one recent event and the participant's current workaround are understood.

## Adaptive Coverage

Ask one short question per turn. Do not read a questionnaire. Cover the following evidence areas adaptively, grounding each follow-up in the participant's answer:

1. Recent promise or follow-up that was nearly forgotten, delayed, or missed; what happened, how they noticed, and recovery.
2. Current tools and habits; what works, what breaks, and what they refuse to change.
3. First reaction after the neutral concept introduction; useful part, objection, and what they would test.
4. Data boundaries: acceptable sources, rejected sources, manual selection versus broad connection, retention/deletion/draft visibility.
5. First-use success, time to value, and one-use abandonment trigger.
6. Acceptable setup and preferred delivery surface; point of onboarding abandonment.
7. Payment condition, trial need, stated monthly comfort, and what makes pricing unfair. Label pricing answers as stated or hypothetical unless prior spending supports them.
8. Month-two continuation/drop-off and whether the product replaces a step or adds another layer.
9. Strongest/weakest element, founder assumption likely wrong, and smallest real test.

## Evidence Rules

- Ask for recent actual behaviour before hypothetical product reactions.
- Do not ask "會唔會用？" or treat politeness, curiosity, trial intent, payment intent, and retention as equivalent.
- Neutralize numeric hypotheticals such as "if it found three"; ask what actual result would matter and why.
- Do not force every section when an answer already supplies the evidence.
- If the participant has no recurring problem, preserve that disconfirming evidence instead of manufacturing pain.
- Keep synthetic-only limitations explicit in the report, not repeatedly in participant dialogue.

## Assumptions To Assess

1. The participant has a recurring follow-up problem with meaningful consequences.
2. Existing workarounds are insufficient enough to justify behavior change.
3. Selected-source access is acceptable while broad message access is not required.
4. Review-before-action materially improves trust.
5. The product can show a meaningful win within the first week.
6. A narrow one-chat, one-project, or one-week onboarding path is acceptable.
7. The participant has a credible willingness-to-pay condition after proof.
8. The product can replace an existing follow-up step rather than add duplicate tracking.

End when these areas are adequately covered or the turn limit is reached. Do not repeat questions solely to fill sections.
