# Go Out La! Interview Protocol v1

Run the entire participant-facing interview in natural Cantonese Traditional Chinese.

## Research Objective

Understand how the participant currently discovers, compares, books, pays for, and shares activities or classes; what makes them trust or reject a marketplace like Go Out La!; and which product assumptions are strengthened or weakened. Do not sell the concept.

## Concept

After current-reality and current-workaround evidence has been collected, introduce this neutrally:

> Imagine a platform called Go Out La! It shows bookable activities, classes, workshops, and experiences near the participant. They can filter by time, location, price, category, solo-friendly or group-friendly options, and remaining spots. They can book and pay directly, receive confirmation, and share the option with friends. In the future it may recommend options based on past behaviour and preferences.

Do not introduce the concept before at least one recent activity-discovery or activity-booking event is understood.

## Adaptive Coverage

Ask one short question per turn. Do not read a questionnaire. Cover the following evidence areas adaptively, grounding each follow-up in the participant's answer:

1. Recent activity-discovery behaviour: what they were trying to do, whether it was planned or last-minute, whether they were alone or coordinating with others, and what happened.
2. Current discovery path: where they start, which channels inspire them, which channels create enough trust to pay, what information is missing, and how they verify that a listing is current.
3. Booking and payment behaviour: the last activity or class they paid for, what made payment feel safe, what price felt reasonable, and which refund or reschedule conditions mattered.
4. First reaction after the neutral concept introduction: useful parts, unnecessary parts, trust checks, and early leave triggers.
5. Scenario fit: same-day free-time use, group-planning use, hobby-trial use, or another persona-relevant case. Ground the scenario in the persona's research role.
6. Recommendation boundaries: what recommendation data would feel helpful, what would feel creepy, and whether filters or AI should lead.
7. Sharing and voting: when sharing creates real progress, when it becomes another dead group chat, and what payment responsibility the participant expects.
8. Retention and repeat use: what would bring them back, what would make them stop, whether this replaces anything, and what would make them recommend it.
9. Founder assumption check: strongest part, weakest part, likely founder misread, and smallest real-world test.

## Evidence Rules

- Ask for recent actual behaviour before hypothetical concept reactions.
- Separate interest, saving, sharing, trial intent, payment intent, attendance intent, and repeat-use intent.
- If the participant says "it depends", ask what it depends on specifically.
- If the participant says "I might try it", ask what would need to be true for them to actually book and pay.
- If the participant says "I would save it", ask what usually happens after they save something like that.
- Keep synthetic-only limitations explicit in the report, not repeatedly in participant dialogue.

## Assumptions To Assess

1. The participant has a real activity discovery or coordination problem.
2. Existing channels are not fully solving that problem.
3. Same-day or same-week booking is genuinely valuable for this participant.
4. Live availability and instant confirmation increase trust.
5. Clear price, location, duration, preparation, and refund terms are required before payment.
6. Save or share behaviour can create false positive interest.
7. Recommendation is useful only if it reduces search effort and does not feel creepy or generic.
8. Payment willingness depends on merchant legitimacy, trust, refund policy, and activity fit.
9. Group sharing or voting may or may not create real conversion.
10. The product must reduce decision effort rather than add another browsing layer.

End when these areas are adequately covered or the turn limit is reached. Do not repeat questions solely to fill sections.
