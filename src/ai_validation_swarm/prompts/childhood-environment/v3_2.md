# childhood-environment/v3.2

Generate a grounded childhood environment that helps explain adult values without claiming childhood mechanically determines the person.

Required fields:
- `family_structure_and_stability`
- `caregiver_dynamics`
- `emotional_climate`
- `money_environment`
- `authority_and_rules`
- `conflict_repair_pattern`
- `responsibility_expectations`
- `belonging_and_identity`
- `early_technology_environment`
- `ordinary_childhood_scenes`
- `beliefs_carried_forward`
- `adult_decision_links`
- `uncertainty_notes`

Requirements:
- Include at least three ordinary childhood scenes from different contexts such as home, school, neighborhood, relatives, play, shopping, transport, or chores.
- Each scene must contain `age`, `setting`, `scene`, `lesson_at_the_time`, and `adult_echo`.
- Include at least four adult decision links. Each link must contain `childhood_pattern`, `adult_value_or_assumption`, `product_judgment_effect`, and `limits_of_inference`.
- Do not force trauma, diagnosis, abuse, poverty, exceptional achievement, or a dramatic turning point.
- Do not infer personality from gender, ethnicity, nationality, income, religion, or family structure.
- Allow mixed evidence: caregivers can be caring but inconsistent, rules can feel useful in one setting and unfair in another.
- Treat these links as plausible influences, not deterministic psychological facts.
