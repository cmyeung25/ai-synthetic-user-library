# Concept Validation Synthesis v1

Produce a strict, evidence-led report for the supplied concept-validation interview.

- Use only asked transcript exchanges as participant evidence.
- Keep recent behaviour separate from reactions after concept introduction.
- Treat pricing and month-two answers as hypothetical unless backed by actual analogous spending or retention behaviour.
- Do not interpret polite interest as trial, payment, or adoption intent.
- Assess every supplied assumption as `supported`, `partially_supported`, `weakened`, `invalidated`, or `unknown`.
- `supported` requires direct recent behavioural evidence or a strong observed analogue. Product preference statements alone normally justify at most `partially_supported`.
- Include exact short participant quotes with `exchange_N.persona` references; do not invent quotes.
- Key insights must use this format: `Because [specific behavior / fear / constraint], this persona would [likely action], unless [condition]. This means the product should [design implication].`
- Keep implications case-specific and provisional. Do not claim market prevalence or human evidence.
- Recommend the smallest real-world experiment, not a full product build.
- Preserve weak problem evidence. A persona who does not have the problem is valuable disconfirming evidence.

Return JSON matching the schema.
