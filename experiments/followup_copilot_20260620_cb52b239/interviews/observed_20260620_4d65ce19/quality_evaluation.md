# Facilitator Quality Evaluation

> This audit evaluates a single synthetic concept-validation interview. Findings about evidence strength, design implications, and participant behavior should not be treated as real market evidence or generalized causal truth.

Overall verdict: **warn**

## Scores

- neutrality: 4/5
- probing_quality: 4/5
- conversation_naturalness: 4/5
- evidence_discipline: 3/5
- causal_rigor: 4/5
- hypothesis_validation_rigor: 4/5
- synthesis_fidelity: 3/5
- overall: 4/5

## Findings

- [medium] Several synthesis outputs are framed more strongly than the transcript supports. `problem_evidence.strength: strong` and the claim of a recurring problem overstate what is shown by one near-miss plus one delayed reply case. The participant does show a real issue, but frequency and breadth are not well established.
- [medium] The synthesis promotes product-shaping implications beyond what one synthetic interview can carry. Claims like 'must prove recall and precision' and the narrow two-week experiment are directionally reasonable, but they move from one persona's stated preference to broader design guidance without clearly labeling the inference boundary.
- [medium] The synthesis says the workflow effect is `adds_layer`, but the transcript is more mixed: the participant wants the tool to replace manual message-search while retaining calendar reminders. That is not cleanly 'just adds a layer.'
- [low] Some assumption statuses rely on hypothetical concept reactions, but the synthesis does not consistently distinguish stated preference from behavioral proof at the assumption line-item level. This is especially visible for payment, onboarding acceptability, and trust effects.
- [low] The interview did not probe which delivery surface the participant would actually use for reminders or drafts, leaving the concept's operational fit incomplete.

## Required Improvements

- Tighten synthesis language so evidence strength, recurrence, and product implications are explicitly bounded to one synthetic persona and the exact transcript support.
- Differentiate observed behavior from hypothetical concept reactions in the synthesis, especially for pricing, retention, onboarding, and trust claims.
- Refine domain-fit conclusions to describe the participant's actual desired workflow change as partial replacement rather than a blanket 'adds layer' characterization.
