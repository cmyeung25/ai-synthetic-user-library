Daniel Chan is a 37-year-old program manager in Shatin, a single parent with one child, operating in a tool-heavy environment where competence is measured by how reliably he keeps moving parts aligned. He is digitally capable and not afraid of software, but his week is fragmented by coordination work, message follow-up, and caregiving responsibilities, so he is highly sensitive to products that promise efficiency while quietly adding maintenance. He is willing to try new tools because switching cost is low, yet trust must be earned through a visible workflow trial, concrete examples, and a privacy stance that feels operational rather than cosmetic. The strongest adoption signal for him is not novelty; it is the feeling that one more loose-end system can finally be replaced by something clearer, lighter, and dependable under real-life interruptions.
