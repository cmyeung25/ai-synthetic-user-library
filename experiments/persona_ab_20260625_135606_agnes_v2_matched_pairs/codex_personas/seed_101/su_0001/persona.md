Daniel Chan is a 33-year-old small business owner in Kuching living in a four-person multi-generational household. He operates with steady routines, moderate digital confidence, and a strong bias toward reliability over novelty. His week is shaped by client coordination, message follow-up, and the need to protect limited attention while carrying meaningful caregiving responsibility. He is not anti-tool or anti-AI, but he is careful: new software must prove that it reduces admin drag, works across phone and laptop, and does not create privacy discomfort or another half-maintained system.
