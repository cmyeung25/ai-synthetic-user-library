# Facilitator Quality Evaluation

> This audit evaluates a synthetic interview only. Any product, segment, pricing, retention, or embedding conclusion should be treated as provisional until checked against real participant evidence.

Overall verdict: **warn**

## Findings

- [medium] The synthesis upgrades one synthetic participant's stated and hypothetical answers into broad retail-customer conclusions and product-shaping claims. Several assumptions are marked `supported` even though the evidence is one persona and much of it comes after concept introduction.
- [medium] The concept introduction preloads both the solution form and a key value dimension: `免費嘅 Portfolio Health Check` that `幫你整合持倉同睇重疊 exposure`. That narrows the response toward the researcher's preferred framing before an open reaction to the concept.
- [medium] Hypothetical answers are sometimes carried forward as if they were workflow or design proof. `retention_risk.workflow_effect` says `replaces_workflow`, but no observed replacement occurred; `payment_justification` and `self-serve/RM` boundaries are also stated preferences, not behavior.
- [low] The final question is mildly compound because it asks both what the participant can understand alone and what is too technical and should be left to RM or reports.
- [medium] The research goal asks how Aladdin-like functions should embed into retail banking services, but the interview only lightly probes embedding context. The synthesis still proposes product-direction outputs such as prototype comparisons and retail-channel boundaries from adjacent evidence.

## Required Improvements

- Reduce synthesis scope: report persona-level signals instead of `supported` retail-customer assumptions from one synthetic concept interview.
- Rewrite concept introduction to avoid seeding `overlap exposure` as the expected primary value before open concept reaction.
- Tag every concept-stage answer as behavioral, stated, or hypothetical and prevent hypothetical statements from being summarized as observed workflow proof.
- Add direct participant-facing probes about embedding context if the output is expected to inform retail-service integration.

## Improvement Hints

- Focus next: Ask the participant to react to a neutral concept description before naming specific analytics outputs.
- Focus next: Probe where this would live in their actual banking journey: homepage, alert, monthly review, RM touchpoint, or standalone tool.
- Focus next: Test what they would do with a concrete output: after seeing overlap or currency concentration, what exact next action would they take or defer?
- Focus next: Ask what makes external-account setup feel worth completing versus too much effort.
- Close gap: Add a participant-facing placement probe to cover service-embedding evidence directly rather than inferring it from reuse timing.
- Close gap: Ask one follow-up that separates `interesting to look at` from `would actually rely on for a decision`.
- Close gap: If pricing remains in scope, ask for a concrete tradeoff or substitute behavior instead of only `what would be worth paying for`.
- Close gap: Probe a failure case: if external accounts are not connected, is the bank-only version still useful enough to open again?
- Prompt change: In concept-validation mode, instruct the facilitator to introduce the concept before naming exemplar features unless the participant already surfaced them unprompted.
- Prompt change: Add a synthesis rule that any single-persona synthetic finding must be explicitly scoped as persona-specific and non-generalizable.
- Prompt change: Add a guardrail that hypothetical statements cannot be converted into `supported` market assumptions without a `stated/hypothetical` label.
- Prompt change: Encourage one explicit embedding question when the research goal includes `how the feature should be embedded`.
- Turn budget: The current turn budget was sufficient for this interview. Keep the soft limit similar, but reserve 1 additional turn for a direct embedding-pattern probe when service-placement decisions are part of the goal.
