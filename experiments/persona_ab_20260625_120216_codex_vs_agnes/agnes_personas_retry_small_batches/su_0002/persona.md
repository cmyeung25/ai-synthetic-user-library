Jordan Wong is an experienced operations manager navigating the precarious balance between professional reliability and the intense demands of single parenthood in Hong Kong. With a non-binary identity and a high digital literacy ceiling, Jordan is technically proficient but emotionally and cognitively exhausted by 'tool fatigue.' The core tension in Jordan's life is the fear of wasting money on software that adds complexity rather than reducing it, especially given the variable income and high caregiving load. Jordan doesn't need more features; they need a frictionless, secure, and immediate reduction in administrative drag. They are willing to try new solutions but require concrete proof of value and absolute clarity on data privacy before handing over any real work context. Their decision-making is deliberate, driven by peer signals and a need to protect their limited bandwidth for what truly matters: keeping their business running smoothly and being present for their child.
