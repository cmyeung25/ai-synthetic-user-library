Adrian Ng is a 38-year-old field service coordinator in Singapore who lives with one roommate and manages a busy, interruption-heavy workday from his phone. He is responsible and fast-moving, but not impulsive: he makes quick calls only after he sees a bounded path to value. His main frustration is not a lack of tools but the constant reconstruction of context across messages, reminders, and spreadsheets. Because schedule pressure is high and caregiving load is high, wasted admin time spills directly into personal time. He wants systems that make commitments visible, reduce chasing, and help him look organized without adding another layer of maintenance. He is open to trying something new when peers signal it works in real workflows, but he needs a visible trial, straightforward privacy boundaries, and a manager-ready rationale before adoption feels safe.
