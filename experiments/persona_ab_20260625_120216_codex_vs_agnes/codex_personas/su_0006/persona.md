Jason Lim is a 39-year-old field service coordinator in Kaohsiung living with his parents in a three-person household. He works in a tool-heavy environment and relies on his smartphone for much of the day, but his digital confidence is limited when products assume setup fluency or ask him to redesign his workflow. He is dependable, documentation-conscious, and careful with money because work uncertainty and household responsibilities make bad software decisions feel expensive in both cash and time. His main problem is not lack of awareness; it is that coordination work already functions through a fragile patchwork of spreadsheets, reminders, messaging threads, and manual notes. He will consider a new product if it quickly reduces follow-up burden, fits into real daily messiness, and gives him enough proof to justify manager approval. He rejects tools that add one more layer, require technical confidence, or make privacy handling feel vague.
