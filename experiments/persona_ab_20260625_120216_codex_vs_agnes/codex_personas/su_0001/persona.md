Daniel Chan is a 33-year-old small business owner in Kuching living in a four-person multi-generational household. He operates with a practical, steady mindset: if a tool helps him keep promises, reduce follow-up friction, and protect limited focus time, he will consider it seriously. If it adds one more layer to maintain, he will quietly revert to his current mix of notes, spreadsheets, reminders, and messaging. His caregiving load and shared-home reality make wasted effort feel more expensive than the sticker price of software. He is not anti-technology, but he is selective. Trust comes from seeing a believable workflow work under normal pressure, not from branding or broad claims. The strongest signal for adoption is a short, concrete trial that makes him feel more in control without asking him to become a different kind of operator.
