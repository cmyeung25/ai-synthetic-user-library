## Additional Interview Summary

Date: 2026-06-24

### Scope

Completed two additional single-person concept interviews:

- `su_2006` Mandy Cheung
- `su_2008` Maggie Leung Wai-ting

Both interviews were adjusted to:

- open with short background / current-role context questions
- stay longer in current-state workflow before concept introduction
- probe more deeply into real decision logic, trust thresholds, and workflow fit

### Mandy Cheung

Session:

- `experiments/ai_user_v5_panel_20260624/single_interview_sessions/observed_20260624_3fee80e1`

Strong signals:

- Her decision bottleneck is not generic roadmap planning. It is repeated judgment about whether onboarding entrance questions ask for trust too early.
- She acts when friction is at the doorway, even before she has a larger sample, if the product is clearly asking for user trust before showing value.
- The platform is most relevant if it helps her decide:
  which setup questions should be removed,
  delayed,
  or justified more clearly.
- She will not trust smooth conclusions alone.
  She wants:
  reasoning path,
  assumptions,
  adjacent-step comparison,
  alternative interpretations,
  and concrete rewrite options.
- Her stop condition is clear:
  if she still has to repair the platform's missing assumptions every time, the tool becomes cleanup work instead of decision support.

Main limitation:

- The session got strong depth on one onboarding use case, but still missed:
  decision ownership,
  public defensibility versus private worry,
  and conflict handling between synthetic output and other evidence.

### Maggie Leung Wai-ting

Session:

- `experiments/ai_user_v5_panel_20260624/single_interview_sessions/observed_20260624_c61e8fc6`

Strong signals:

- Her core filter is behavioral hesitation, not surface-level positive feedback.
- She watches for the moment users need to "fill in the story themselves" before they can proceed.
- The platform is most relevant if it can detect where users must self-justify, preserve contradiction, and keep minority or edge-case reactions visible.
- She does not want a clean story too early.
  She wants:
  traceability from raw material to conclusion,
  explicit contradiction,
  separation of minority views,
  and evidence that the system is not treating every objection as equally high risk.
- Her preferred workflow insertion point is before formal synthesis or before real-user research planning:
  as a pre-map, not as review-ready evidence.

Main limitation:

- The session was stronger on synthesis and prototype-review preparation than on roadmap or feature-priority tradeoffs in the broader sense.
- It still missed a direct probe on:
  what she could defend publicly,
  what still felt privately uncertain,
  and one clearer scope / sequencing tradeoff outcome.

### Cross-Interview Takeaways

- The opening background questions were useful.
  They made the conversation feel more natural and gave better role context before the real decision probe.
- The deeper version worked better than the first Janice run on trust and workflow insertion.
- Even so, the facilitator still tends to close too early once generic coverage is marked complete.
- The biggest remaining method gap is not turn budget.
  It is coverage discipline against the actual research goal.

### Recommended Next Move

Before treating these as a full three-person concept panel, tighten the interview rules again:

- require one explicit question on:
  public defensibility versus private worry
- require one explicit question on:
  conflicting evidence handling
- require one explicit question on:
  what current step gets replaced or shortened
- do not allow concept introduction until at least one concrete tradeoff, one missing-evidence point, and one pressure point are surfaced
