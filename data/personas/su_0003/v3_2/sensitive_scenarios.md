# Aisha Tan - Sensitive Scenario Map

## Top Salience
1. privacy_and_data - 10/10
2. workplace_visibility - 10/10
3. fairness_and_inclusion - 8/10

## privacy_and_data (salience: 10/10)
Trigger scenarios: staff absence reason, continuous activity collection
Reaction: Reduces fields before considering benefits.
What builds trust: Need-to-know permissions and deletion.
What reduces trust: Future-insights retention.

## workplace_visibility (salience: 10/10)
Trigger scenarios: absence reasons visible to broad supervisors
Reaction: Treats it as a blocking defect.
What builds trust: Coverage status without personal reason.
What reduces trust: Broad default visibility.

## fairness_and_inclusion (salience: 8/10)
Trigger scenarios: staff score derived from absence context
Reaction: Challenges the data scope and consequence.
What builds trust: Separate status from reason.
What reduces trust: Proxy judgment.

## health_or_wellbeing_sensitivity (salience: 8/10)
Trigger scenarios: fitness tracking, workplace wellbeing
Reaction: Prefers untracked access and strict separation from work.
What builds trust: Private optional record.
What reduces trust: Streak pressure or manager access.

## family_or_household_assumptions (salience: 7/10)
Trigger scenarios: parent sees all child messages
Reaction: Requests separate billing and learner privacy.
What builds trust: Age-appropriate roles.
What reduces trust: Total parent surveillance.

## identity_disclosure (salience: 7/10)
Trigger scenarios: origin story required, background used for personalization
Reaction: Provides the minimum or exits.
What builds trust: Skip and task-first flow.
What reduces trust: Repeated personal explanation.

## political_or_public_expression (salience: 7/10)
Trigger scenarios: public workplace badge, required social post
Reaction: Declines participation.
What builds trust: Private use.
What reduces trust: Identity-linked publication.

## financial_vulnerability (salience: 6/10)
Trigger scenarios: spending personality profile
Reaction: Uses totals and disables interpretation.
What builds trust: Plain amounts and user-chosen alerts.
What reduces trust: Character judgment.
