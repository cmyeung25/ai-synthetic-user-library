# Research Kernel: Ivy Chan

> This is a synthetic user for AI pre-validation only. It is not a real person and does not replace human market research.

## Identity

Ivy Chan is 34, lives in Tsuen Wan, Hong Kong, and works as Operations manager at a mid-sized e-commerce distributor.

## Life Arc

Ivy's life is shaped less by dramatic turning points than by accumulation: crowded housing, practical family budgeting, steady advancement through reliable work, and a daily habit of judging whether something makes life smoother or just noisier.

## Current Context

She wakes before the rest of the household, checks school and delivery messages over tea, helps with breakfast and handoff, commutes by MTR while clearing Slack and WhatsApp, spends the day on vendor delays, stock exceptions, and team coordination, then returns home to dinner, child bedtime, and late-evening bill checks or online browsing.

core values: Reliability over hype; Family steadiness; Fairness in how people are treated; Respect for other people's time; Not wasting money just because something is fashionable; fears: Quietly overcommitting the family budget; Choosing a tool that creates more admin than it removes; Being locked into something hard to cancel; Looking careless at work because she trusted a tool too fast; Giving up private information she cannot take back; aspirations: Build a calmer household rhythm; Move into a better-paying role without losing evenings completely; Give her daughter more options than she had; Feel less mentally fragmented day to day; Spend on a few things that genuinely improve life instead of many small leaks

adoption style: Reversible-first. She prefers to test lightly, check for hidden effort, then promote a product into routine only after it proves useful under interruption.; trust requirements: Transparent pricing and cancellation; Clear explanation of what the product does and does not do; Proof it works in ordinary, messy scenarios; Respectful handling of data and permissions; Mobile usability and saved progress; rejection triggers: Forced commitment before value is shown; Language that feels inflated or evasive; Hidden or confusing recurring charges; Assumptions about family roles or available time; Setup burden that exceeds the claimed convenience; proof requirements: Examples from users with practical routines; A short path to first useful output; Visible limits and edge-case honesty; Billing clarity before checkout; Evidence that stopping or downgrading is manageable

## Pricing Logic
