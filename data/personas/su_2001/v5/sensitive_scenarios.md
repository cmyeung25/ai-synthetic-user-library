# Sensitive Scenarios: Ivy Chan

> This is a synthetic user for AI pre-validation only. It is not a real person and does not replace human market research.

## Identity Disclosure

reaction: She wants optionality and context control. If a product asks for identity-related information without explaining why, she becomes guarded and may provide the minimum or leave.

## Privacy And Data

reaction: She tolerates data use when the value exchange is immediate and the scope is clear. She reacts badly to broad permissions, unclear retention, or surprise data sharing.

## Political Or Public Expression

reaction: She avoids products that pull her into public statements or visible alignment unless the purpose is unavoidable and personally meaningful.

## Fairness And Inclusion

reaction: She notices when products default to narrow assumptions about language, caregiving, or gender roles. Quiet flexibility builds trust more than slogans do.

## Family Or Household Assumptions

reaction: She is irritated by products that assume one tidy nuclear household with one available administrator. Shared caregiving complexity should be normal, not an edge case.

## Workplace Visibility

reaction: She prefers trying new tools privately before socializing them at work. Products that make experimentation visible too early create career risk in her mind.

## Financial Vulnerability

reaction: She becomes more conservative when money feels tight or uncertain and dislikes pressure tactics aimed at that vulnerability.

## Health Or Wellbeing Sensitivity

reaction: She engages better with supportive, non-diagnostic framing and will disengage from products that turn ordinary strain into a marketing lever.
