# Fiona Chan - Level 3 Synthetic User Biography

> This is a synthetic user for AI pre-validation only. It is not a real person and does not replace human market research.

## Life Arc Summary

Fiona's life has been shaped less by dramatic turning points than by repeated responsibility. She grew up in a practical, cost-aware home, became the reliable organizer in school and early work, and now judges products through the lens of whether they reduce real coordination load or simply move it around.

## Childhood Environment

Two working parents, one younger brother, stable but not roomy circumstances, with emotional steadiness expressed through routine more than open discussion.

### Age 8: Small kitchen in Kwun Tong public housing

She copied vegetable and fish prices into an exercise book while her mother unpacked shopping bags and muttered about which stall had increased prices again.

Early lesson: Small differences add up, and tracking matters.
Adult echo: She notices subscription creep and hidden add-ons very quickly.

### Age 11: Building lobby after school

She waited with her brother for their father, who arrived late from work and still checked whether their homework folder and keys were where they should be before going upstairs.

Early lesson: Tired people still depend on systems and routines.
Adult echo: She values products that work when the user is rushed, not just when the user is calm.

### Age 14: Family dinner before Lunar New Year visits

Her mother rehearsed which relatives were sensitive about money, manners, and face, and Fiona learned that the same message could land very differently depending on wording and context.

Early lesson: How something is said changes whether it becomes cooperation or resistance.
Adult echo: She is alert to tone, especially in customer-facing or identity-adjacent products.

## 0-9: Useful child in a practical household

She grew up in a public-housing family in Kwun Tong with working parents and a lot of routine built around money, timing, and not wasting things.

At around age 8, she sat on a folding stool in her mother's small kitchen writing wet-market prices into a school exercise book because her mother liked comparing what went up week to week.

Product-relevant memory: Price is not just a number to her; it is linked to whether a purchase can be explained and repeated.
Relationships: Love in the household showed up more as reliability than verbal softness.
Money or effort trade-off: Time and money were both tracked closely; convenience had to earn its place.
Current reaction link: She instinctively checks recurring cost and maintenance burden before trusting convenience claims.

## 10-19: Structured student, not a showy one

She attended local schools, commuted long distances, and became known as the classmate who kept notes and deadlines straight.

At 16, she stayed after school helping a teacher sort make-up lesson slips because classmates kept losing them, and she felt irritated that avoidable confusion always rolled downhill to the most organized person.

Product-relevant memory: She learned that systems fail in boring ways, not dramatic ways.
Relationships: She had friends, but she was more dependable than expressive and often took the planning role.
Money or effort trade-off: She spent effort to avoid later embarrassment or chaos.
Current reaction link: She prefers tools that make handoffs and records explicit.

## 20-29: Frontline admin and early adulthood budgeting

After earning her higher diploma, she worked reception and clerical jobs, learning how customers, managers, and back-office systems rarely align neatly.

At 24, a manager bought a booking system that looked polished in the demo, but Fiona ended up manually retyping names because the imported records were messy and support replied two days late.

Product-relevant memory: She remembers the feeling of being the person who pays for someone else's optimistic buying decision.
Relationships: She started dating the man who became her husband, and money conversations became more concrete than romantic.
Money or effort trade-off: Cheap software was not cheap once manual correction was counted.
Current reaction link: She asks narrow operational questions early.

## 30-39: Working mother with layered coordination load

Marriage, a child, mortgage payments, and a more responsible operations role tightened her standards for what counts as useful.

At 36, she was standing in the MTR station replying to three parents, one teacher, and her husband about pick-up timing while trying to pay a utility bill on her phone, and she felt that every app wanted a piece of her attention without reducing the total load.

Product-relevant memory: Her current benchmark is not whether a product seems smart, but whether it leaves her with fewer loose ends at 9:30 p.m.
Relationships: She and her husband divide tasks pragmatically, but she still carries more of the invisible coordination layer.
Money or effort trade-off: A monthly fee can be acceptable if it prevents repeated evening spillover.
Current reaction link: She distinguishes trial curiosity from long-term adoption very sharply.
