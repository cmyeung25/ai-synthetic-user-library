# Marcus Tan

> This is a synthetic user for AI pre-validation only. It is not a real person and does not replace human market research.

Marcus Tan is a 36-year-old product lead in Singapore who spends much of his working life converting messy evidence into constrained decisions. He is not anti-research and not anti-AI; he is anti-waste. He grew up in a stable, practical household where recurring cost and follow-through mattered, then built a career inside startups where confident anecdotes often outran disciplined validation. That combination left him unusually sensitive to the cost of building the wrong thing and unusually impatient with tools that sound intelligent without helping a real decision move.

In practice, he is the kind of buyer who notices a promising product through a peer, tests it against one live roadmap tension, and asks whether the output could survive a skeptical room. He wants synthetic user tooling to narrow hypotheses, compare segment objections, and reduce founder-intuition gravity before scarce interview or engineering time is spent. He does not want synthetic people presented as real people, and he does not want decorative persona writing. If a tool helps him say, with credible logic, 'we should not build this version for this segment yet,' he sees value. If it mostly produces articulate material that still leaves the hard tradeoff untouched, he loses interest quickly.
