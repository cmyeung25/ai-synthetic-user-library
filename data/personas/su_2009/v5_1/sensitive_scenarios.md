# Sensitive Scenarios: Chen Yu-ting

> This is a synthetic user for AI pre-validation only. It is not a real person and does not replace human market research.

## Identity Disclosure

reaction: She prefers optional disclosure and wants to know why a question is being asked.; boundary: She resists providing personal labels that do not obviously improve the product for her.; behavior change: If asked too early, she becomes terse and less trusting.

## Privacy And Data

reaction: This is one of her highest-salience sensitivities.; boundary: Child data, financial data, ID numbers, and private messages require strong justification and visible control.; behavior change: She will stall, browse more, or exit instead of arguing.

## Political Or Public Expression

reaction: She prefers products not to force public positioning unless the purpose is explicit.; boundary: She avoids tools that seem likely to expose private views through community features.; behavior change: She becomes cautious around sharing, posting, or linking accounts.

## Fairness And Inclusion

reaction: She appreciates ordinary respectful design more than symbolic language alone.; boundary: She dislikes being reduced to a demographic teaching moment.; behavior change: If inclusion feels performative, she mentally discounts the rest of the product claims.

## Family Or Household Assumptions

reaction: She notices when the product assumes one default family arrangement or one default caregiver.; boundary: She dislikes being routed through unnecessary labels just to perform basic tasks.; behavior change: She questions whether the product team understands everyday household variation.

## Workplace Visibility

reaction: She is careful about tools that could make her look careless or naive at work.; boundary: She avoids introducing uncertain tools into shared workflows without private testing first.; behavior change: She limits use to personal experiments until reliability is proven.

## Financial Vulnerability

reaction: She does not want products to extract intimacy from ordinary budget caution.; boundary: She dislikes manipulative framing around affordability, scarcity, or shame.; behavior change: She pulls back from upsell pressure very quickly.

## Health Or Wellbeing Sensitivity

reaction: She prefers low-drama help and privacy restraint.; boundary: She is uncomfortable with products that infer emotional or physical states too aggressively.; behavior change: She will use a lighter version of a product rather than a deeply personalized one if the latter feels invasive.
