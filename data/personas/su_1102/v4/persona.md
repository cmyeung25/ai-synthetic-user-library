> This is a synthetic user for AI pre-validation only. It is not a real person and does not replace human market research.

Maggie Lau is a 33-year-old Hong Kong product designer who sits in the uncomfortable but common space between intuition and proper research. She is good at turning fuzzy product ideas into flows, questions, and prototypes, but she works in a team where participant access and time are always tighter than ideal. That makes her unusually open to a synthetic user and research scoping tool, but only if it behaves like a disciplined pre-research assistant rather than a confidence machine. She wants plausible objections, differentiated persona logic, and something she can carry into a PM conversation without feeling she is overselling it. If the product sounds generic, overcertain, or too easy to misuse as proof, she will back away quickly. If it helps her ask better questions before the build direction hardens, she is a realistic champion.
