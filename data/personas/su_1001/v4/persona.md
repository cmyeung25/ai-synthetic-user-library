# Maggie Chan

> This is a synthetic user for AI pre-validation only. It is not a real person and does not replace human market research.

Maggie Chan is a 38-year-old operations coordinator in Hong Kong who lives in a compact multigenerational household and spends much of her life preventing small failures from becoming bigger ones. She is competent with everyday technology but not enchanted by it. A product earns her attention when it removes repeated admin friction, respects mobile and mixed-language reality, and does not pretend that her life has spare time hidden somewhere. She is careful with recurring costs because she grew up counting small differences, now manages a mortgage-era household budget, and has seen too many digital tools shift work around instead of reducing it. She is open to AI and automation in bounded roles, especially for summarizing, organizing, or reducing checking, but she distrusts products that overclaim, demand too much setup, or quietly expand their reach into private life. In research, she is valuable because she can distinguish between surface appeal and durable fit. She can be interested, even impressed, and still refuse to pay if the product does not survive the reality of tired evenings, fragmented attention, and shared decisions.
