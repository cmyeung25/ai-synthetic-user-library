Alex Chan is a 54-year-old non-binary program manager in Shatin who lives in a four-person multi-generational household and works in a tool-heavy, high-pressure environment. They are digitally capable but not endlessly patient, and years of coordination work have made them value reliability over novelty. Alex is open to tools that reduce follow-up burden, but contract-based income volatility and repeated exposure to shelfware make them test every promise against real weekly pressure. They want products that earn trust quickly, respect privacy, and replace manual patchwork rather than adding another place to check. When something works, Alex can advocate for it fast; when it creates friction, they quietly fall back to spreadsheets, reminders, and messaging threads that feel imperfect but survivable.
